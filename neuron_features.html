01
Core knowledge engine
The foundation. Ingests everything, structures it, verifies it, makes it actionable.
MVP · Build first
⚡

Universal ingestion

Connects silently to every tool a company uses — Slack, Notion, Google Drive, Jira, Linear, GitHub, email, call recordings — and reads updates in real time. No manual entry ever required. Setup takes under 5 minutes per integration.

Slack APINotion APIGoogle DriveJiraGitHubGmail
🧩

Structured rule extraction

Instead of just storing documents, Neuron reads them and extracts the underlying decisions and rules: what the rule is, who owns it, under what conditions it applies, and what the exceptions are. Output is structured JSON, not a document summary.

Input: "Refunds over $500 need Sarah's approval" in a Slack thread
Output: { rule: "refund_approval", threshold: 500, owner: "Sarah K.", team: "support" }
✅

Conflict detection and verification

When two sources say different things about the same topic, Neuron detects the conflict automatically, freezes that rule from agent use, and routes a one-click verification request to the relevant owner via Slack DM. Nobody acts on stale or contradictory information.

Notion doc says 14-day trial. CEO Slack message says 30 days. → Neuron pings both, freezes the rule, updates once confirmed.
🔍

Natural language search

Any team member searches in plain English and gets a single verified answer — with source, owner, and last-verified date — in under one second. Not a list of documents to read through. Not an AI guess. A sourced fact.

"Who approves contracts over $50K?" → "Marcus T., VP Sales. Approved via #deal-desk. Last verified May 2026."
🔄

Auto-updating knowledge graph

Every time a decision is made in Slack, a doc is updated in Notion, or a policy changes anywhere — Neuron picks it up, extracts the change, and flags the owner to verify. The knowledge map is always current. No maintenance burden on the team.

real-time syncchange detectionowner alerts
🤖

Agent-executable API (MCP)

AI agents query Neuron via a simple REST or MCP API before taking any action. They get structured, verified context — rule, confidence score, owner, timestamp, source — and can execute workflows reliably instead of hallucinating company-specific facts.

Agent: GET /query?q=refund_policy&amount=320 → { rule: "approve", confidence: 0.97, owner: "support-team", verified: "2026-05-01" }
REST APIMCP protocolJSON outputconfidence scoring
02
Real-time conversation monitoring
Neuron watches live conversations and takes action automatically — without being asked.
MVP · Core differentiator
🐛

Bug mention detection → owner notification

When a bug or system issue is mentioned in any connected channel, Neuron identifies the relevant component, maps it to its owner in the knowledge graph, and sends them a contextual ping — without any manual tagging or @mention required.

Alex + Marie discuss Safari crash on payment modal → Neuron DMs John: "Alex and Marie flagged a Safari crash on checkout step 3 — your area. Might be related to your work."
SlackDiscordTeamscomponent ownership map
📋

Implicit task extraction → instant assignment

When someone says "remind John to finish X by 5pm" in a conversation — even casually — Neuron detects it, extracts the task, creates it, assigns it to John, sets a reminder, and confirms in the thread. No task manager needed.

"Someone remind John about the API migration by 5pm" → Neuron DMs John: "Alex and Marie asked me to remind you: finish the API migration by 5:00 PM. Reminder set for 4:30 PM."
person detectiondeadline extractionauto-remindertask list sync
📌

Decision capture from conversations

When a decision is made in a Slack thread or meeting transcript — even informally — Neuron detects it, drafts a structured rule, and routes it to the decision-maker for one-click confirmation before adding it to the knowledge graph.

CEO: "Let's go with 30-day trials from now on" in Slack → Neuron: "Detected decision: trial length changed to 30 days. Confirm to add to knowledge base?" [Confirm] [Edit]
📣

Mention & relevance routing

When a topic, person, project, or system is discussed and Neuron knows it's relevant to someone who isn't in the conversation, it sends a quiet, non-intrusive heads-up — so nothing important falls through the cracks due to people being in different channels.

#product discusses a change that affects the auth service → Neuron quietly notifies the auth service owner with a one-line summary and a link.
03
Onboarding & offboarding
New hires get productive in days, not months. Departures stop destroying institutional knowledge.
MVP · Fastest ROI to show
🚀

New hire knowledge companion

From day one, every new hire has a personal Neuron companion that answers any "how do we..." question instantly with verified, sourced answers. No interrupting senior teammates. No searching Slack history for hours. No getting the wrong answer from an outdated doc.

Day 1, 9am: "What's the process for deploying to production?" → step-by-step verified runbook, owner listed, last updated 3 days ago.
Slack botweb search UIrole-based context
📚

Role-specific knowledge packages

When a new hire joins, Neuron automatically compiles a curated package of the most important verified knowledge for their specific role — policies, processes, ownership maps, and key contacts — delivered on day one without any manual preparation.

New support hire joins → automatically receives: refund policies, escalation process, tool access guide, team contact map. All verified, all current.
⚠️

Offboarding knowledge risk alert

When someone is flagged as leaving (via HR system or manually), Neuron identifies every knowledge item where they are the sole verified owner, ranks them by criticality, and alerts the team with a prioritized list and suggested replacements — before their last day.

Alex (Eng Lead) leaving Friday → Neuron alert: "14 items need new owners. Critical: deployment checklist (daily use). High: oncall runbook (incident use). Suggested new owner: Priya."
HR system integrationcriticality scoringowner suggestions
🔁

Knowledge transfer workflows

One-click to reassign ownership of knowledge items during offboarding. New owner receives a verification request for each item, prompted to review, confirm, or update. Ensures nothing is rubber-stamped and everything is actually validated before the person leaves.

04
Idea capture & strategic alignment
Ideas discussed in Slack threads stop disappearing. Neuron catches them, evaluates them, and connects them to company goals.
Post-MVP · Phase 2
💡

Passive idea detection from conversations

Neuron listens for idea signals across all channels — proposals, suggestions, "what if we..." statements — and captures them automatically into a structured idea log without anyone needing to manually save or document anything.

"What if we added a referral program?" in #product → automatically saved to Product Ideas log with author, date, channel, and context.
🎯

OKR and mission alignment scoring

Every captured idea is automatically cross-referenced against the company's current OKRs, strategic priorities, and mission — and gets an alignment score. High-fit ideas surface to the top. Low-fit ideas still get saved but don't create noise.

Referral program idea → "Fits Q3 growth OKR: reduce CAC. Referral programs typically lower CAC by 30–50% when incentive matches core product value. Alignment: high."
OKR syncmission matchingrelevance scoring
🕰️

Historical context surfacing

Before an idea is captured, Neuron checks whether anything similar was tried before — and if so, surfaces what happened, who was involved, and why it succeeded or failed. Prevents teams from repeating experiments without knowing the history.

"We tried a referral program 2 years ago — drop-off after month 2. No record of why in the knowledge base." → flags gap for the team to investigate before proceeding.
✨

Idea enrichment and improvement suggestions

Neuron doesn't just store ideas — it improves them. After capturing an idea, it generates 1–2 specific suggestions for how to strengthen it based on company context, known constraints, and historical patterns.

Referral program idea → "Suggestion: tie the reward to activated usage (not just signup) to address the drop-off risk. This also aligns with your activation metric in the Q3 OKR."
05
PR & engineering assistant
New engineers get CTO-level context from day one. Senior engineers stop being interrupted for the same questions.
Post-MVP · High value for eng teams
🔎

PR problem pattern matching

When an engineer opens a PR or posts an error in a dev channel, Neuron scans the entire history of past PRs, Slack dev threads, and resolved issues to find the closest matching problem — and surfaces how it was solved, by whom, and what was left undone.

New hire: "Getting 'undefined userId' on auth middleware." → Neuron: "This happened in PR #287. CTO Max fixed it with a null check on line 34 of auth.middleware.ts. Here's the exact fix."
GitHub PR historySlack dev threadserror pattern matching
📝

Unfinished task detection from past PRs

Engineers frequently leave "TODO" comments, unwritten tests, and deferred improvements in PRs. Neuron tracks all of these across the codebase and surfaces them when a related area is touched — so technical debt is visible, not hidden.

Max's PR #287 comment: "Add a test for this — it'll break if session init order changes." → That test was never written. Neuron surfaces this when the auth area is touched again.
👤

Codebase ownership map

Neuron builds and maintains a living map of who owns what in the codebase — based on actual PR history and commit patterns, not manually assigned labels. When a related area is touched, the right person is automatically surfaced as a suggested reviewer.

PR touches payment-modal component → "Max is the historical owner of this area (23 PRs). Auto-adding as suggested reviewer."
GitHub integrationcommit analysisauto-reviewer suggestions
🏛️

Architecture decision log

Every significant architectural decision — why a library was chosen, why a pattern was used, what alternatives were considered — is automatically extracted from PR descriptions, Slack discussions, and meeting notes into a searchable decision log.

"Why are we using Redis instead of Postgres for sessions?" → "Max's decision, PR #156: Redis chosen for sub-5ms session reads at scale. Revisit if we drop below 5K DAU."
06
Clone agent
The long-term moat. Neuron learns how each person works — and preserves that intelligence permanently.
Future · 12–18 months · Biggest moat
🧬

Individual working pattern learning

Over time, Neuron builds a deep model of how each person works: their coding style and patterns, how they structure decisions, how they communicate feedback, what they prioritize, how they debug. This builds passively — no effort required from the person.

coding patternsdecision stylecommunication tonepriority patternsdebugging instincts
👻

Departure-proof knowledge preservation

When a key person leaves, their clone agent remains. It can answer questions about their codebase areas, explain their architectural decisions, suggest how they would approach a new problem, and surface everything they left unfinished — indefinitely.

"How would Max approach refactoring the session layer?" → Clone agent answers based on 3 years of learned patterns: "He always isolates first, writes tests before refactoring, leaves 'why' comments on non-obvious choices."
🔗

New hire continuity handoff

When a new person takes over a departed employee's role, Neuron gives them full access to the clone agent for that role — so they inherit context, patterns, open decisions, and institutional knowledge from day one, continuing at the same level without disruption.

New eng lead inherits Max's clone agent → gets immediate context on all owned systems, ongoing decisions, unresolved TODOs, and Max's typical approach to new problems in those areas.
📈

Compounding intelligence over time

Every month Neuron runs, every clone agent gets richer. After 2 years, a company's accumulated intelligence becomes so deep and specific that it's genuinely irreplaceable — not just useful. This is the moat that makes switching cost approach infinity.

Year 1: Neuron knows your policies. Year 2: Neuron knows how every key person thinks. Year 3: Neuron IS your institutional memory. Switching means starting from zero.
